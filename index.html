Travel to Iceland

Places to Visit

  • Silfra
  • Skaftafell Ice Caves
  • Kirkjufell

Silfra


Touch the edges of the Eurasian and North American plate at the same time when diving in the clear waters of Silfra. The water, being almost completely transparent, refracts light in a similar manner that crystals do. The stunning landscape is composed of lava rock, sand, and algae and attracts snorklers from all over the world. Silfra can be enjoyed all year, whether it is summer or winter. Either way, the waters of the rift can reach 2° Celsius so make sure to bring a jacket!

Skaftafell Ice Caves


Visible after rain has fallen in winter, the Skaftafell ice caves are composed of highly pressurized glacier ice that is centuries old. The caves are created in glaciers, which means they are not fixed structures. Winter is the best time to visit since the ice of the caves may not be hard enough in any other season. The most famous of the ice caves is the Crystal Ice Cave, known for its turquoise, aquamarine, and quartz-like structures. Visit the Skaftafell Ice Caves quickly because the increasing global temperature is melting these caverns.

Kirkjufell


Named for its likeness to the shape of a church steeple, Kirkjufell is an attraction that maintains its beauty all year. One can find the northern lights and midnight sun at Kirkjufell, which can only be found in certain places. You might recognize Kirkjufell because a scene from Game of Thrones was taken here. Make sure to bring your cameras because this scenery is instagram quality!

The Best Authentic Cuisine

  1. Hákarl
  2. Svið
  3. Súrsaðir Hrútspungar

Hákarl


Perhaps one of the most iconic dishes of Icelandic cuisine, Hákarl is shark that is fermented by burying the body in the dirt and hanging it up to dry. In the past, Hákarl was made using urine for fermentation. Hakarl is usually eaten with a type of spirit called "Brennivín."

Svið


A traditional Icelandic dish, a sheep's head isn't unusual for Icelandic people. According to the people, the cheek is the best part of the head. Like any cooked animal, some people even eat the eyes. Svið can be cooked in numerous ways from boiling to roasting. Usually, this dish is served with a side of mashed potatoes and rutabaga jelly.

Súrsaðir Hrútspungar


This delicacy is made with the testicles of a ram, and is enjoyed by the locals of Iceland. Súrsaðir Hrútspungar is prepared many ways, but the most popular way is to pair the ram's testicles with whey and gelatin. While it may not sound appetizing, it is definitely popular.

Experience Icelandic Culture First Hand at Home


In this game of Fox and Geese, one player uses 15 white pieces, which represent geese, while the other player uses 1 red or black piece, which represent the fox. The objective of the geese is to capture the fox, but can only do so by moving sideways, diagonally forward, or straight forward. The fox must evade immobilization by capturing at least 13 geese.
